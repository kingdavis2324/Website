About Us

What is Urban Nations Outreach?

Urban Nations Outreach is an evangelistic ministry that focuses on ministering to Muslims and other "10/40 Window" immigrant groups in New York City

UNO has developed ongoing ministries reaching out to the following New York immigrant groups:
West African Muslims (from several countries and tribes) and South Asians (from Bangladesh, Pakistan, India, Nepal & Tibet).

We now have two evangelical storefront Outreach Centers in New York City:

THE SOUTH ASIAN CENTER in Jackson Heights, Queens

THE WEST AFRICAN CENTER in Bedford-Stuyvesant, Brooklyn

URBAN NATIONS OUTREACH is a 501c3, Non-profit ministry guided by a Board of Directors. The Board members are mainly New york pastors and laypeople who have a strong burden for ministering to Muslims and the "10/40 Window". Pastor Tom Richter (New Hope Christian Church, SBC) serves as the president of the Board.

CHURCHES (local and from across the country) help UNO minister to Unreached People Group immigrants by contributing financially and by sending volunteers to help teach ESL and do other ministry among the immigrants.

YOUTH & ADULT MISSION TEAMS come to New York throughout the year to help with ESL, children's ministry, evangelism, and construction/repair projects. The teams come from all over the U.S., as well as Canada and Europe.

URBAN NATIONS MISSIONARIES serve short and long terms (anywhere from 1 week to 2 years). Missionaries help with all of our daily outreach programs at our ministry centers. In addition to the regular programs, each missionary is matched up with specific immigrants who are open for one-on-one discipleship. Some UNO missionaries or interns serve in more specific roles such as church planter, worship leader, building maintenance/construction, childrens worker, etc. Most missionaries and interns also help guide local volunteers and visiting mission teams to minister in the city throughout the year.

Larry Holcomb is the founder and director of Urban Nations Outreach, New York. He has been involved with urban ministry for more than 25 years, and as a denominational strategist, played a key role in the initial planning stages of successful churches in New York such as: New Hope, Journey, Crossroads, All Nations Hispanic Baptist, & Beach Church. Larry, his wife Linda, and their kids, live in the UNO Missionary House in Woodhaven....an urban immigrant neighborhood in the Queens section of New York City. He is a frequent guest speaker at churches and conferences across the country.

Great love for NYC!
